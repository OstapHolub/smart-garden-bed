# Planter Box Design

## Status: box completed, liner and conduits still pending

The removable planter box sits in the mounting frame that's part of the
already-finished wooden base. As of this writing:

- ✅ Box construction (frame, bottom panel) completed and fitted to the
  base's mounting frame.
- ✅ All box parts measured, cut, and sanded through three grits (see
  [`../docs/timeline.md`](../docs/timeline.md), Day 8).
- ✅ Two coats of oil finish applied and the box assembled (Day 9–10).
- ✅ Sizing mistake found and fixed: the box was built 4 cm too wide
  (99 × 53 cm against a 100 × 50 cm mounting frame, target was
  99 × 49 cm) — confirmed by test-fitting against the base. Trimmed,
  reassembled, and confirmed to fit the mounting frame — see
  [`../docs/timeline.md`](../docs/timeline.md), Day 10–11, and
  [`../docs/lessons-learned.md`](../docs/lessons-learned.md).
- 🚧 Geotextile liner installation is planned.
- 📋 Sensor conduit routing is being planned but not yet built — see
  [`sensor-conduits.md`](sensor-conduits.md).

## Design intent

- The box must lift out of the base's mounting frame as a single unit, so
  soil can be removed and the box stored separately (e.g., over winter).
- The box is being dimensioned against the base's actual built mounting
  frame, not against the original Fusion 360 target dimensions — see
  [`../mechanical/cut-list.md`](../mechanical/cut-list.md) for why.
- At least two cable conduits (PVC or electrical conduit) are planned
  into the box: one for the active sensor cable bundle, one spare for
  future expansion.

## Not yet finalized

- Bottom panel drainage approach (see
  [`drainage-and-liner.md`](drainage-and-liner.md)).
- Finish product — likely the same oil used on the base, not yet
  confirmed.
