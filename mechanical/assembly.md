# Base Assembly Notes

## Sequence (as built)

1. Cut lumber to length; dry-fit the frame shape to check proportions.
2. Sand all boards through multiple grits.
3. Disassemble and hand-label every part on the end grain (position and
   orientation) before finishing — done specifically to avoid confusing
   near-identical boards during reassembly.
4. Apply two coats of oil finish, with drying time between coats, to the
   main frame boards and separately to the legs.
5. Reassemble using the end-grain labels as a guide.
6. Attach the lower shelf (built from individual slats).
7. Move the assembled base into its final position on the terrace.

See [`../docs/timeline.md`](../docs/timeline.md) for the day-by-day log
and photos, and [`../docs/lessons-learned.md`](../docs/lessons-learned.md)
for why parts were labeled before finishing.

## Fastening approach

Most fasteners are driven from the inside faces or from underneath, so
they're not visible when looking at the assembled base from the outside.
The top corners of the frame are an exception: small metal corner
brackets are used there and are visible in the finished photos
(`../docs/photos/final/`). This was a deliberate trade-off — those corners
take the most structural load, and the brackets were judged worth the
visible fasteners.

## Known issue: switch clearance

The base's final position on the terrace is close to a wall-mounted light
switch. This is visible in the Day 7 photos
(`../docs/photos/assembly/d07-assembled-base-in-place.jpg`). It didn't
block assembly, but it's a constraint worth keeping in mind when
positioning the planter box and any future electronics enclosure.
