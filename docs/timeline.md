# Timeline

Build log for the wooden base, reconstructed from build photos. Days are
relative build-session numbers (Day 1, Day 2, ...), not calendar dates —
this project wasn't built on consecutive calendar days, and exact dates
weren't logged during the build.

## Day 1 — Cutting lumber, first frame shape

- Cut lumber to length on a miter saw at a portable workbench.
- Dry-assembled the first frame shape to check proportions before any
  finishing work.
- Photos: [`photos/woodworking/d01-first-cut.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d01-first-cut.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d01-first-shape.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d01-first-shape.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d01-results.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d01-results.jpg).

## Day 2 — Surface prep

- Compared a raw board against a sanded one to judge how much surface prep
  the lumber needed before finishing.
- Photos: [`photos/woodworking/d02-raw-board.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d02-raw-board.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d02-sanded-board.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d02-sanded-board.jpg).

## Day 3 — First oil coat

- Applied the first coat of oil finish to the main boards.
- Photos: [`photos/woodworking/d03-oiling-board-1.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d03-oiling-board-1.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d03-oiling-board-2.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d03-oiling-board-2.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d03-oiling-board-results.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d03-oiling-board-results.jpg).

## Day 4 — Disassembly, labeling, second coat

- Disassembled the base and hand-labeled every part on the end grain
  (orientation, position — e.g. "top left crossbar", "center crossbar")
  before continuing finishing work, specifically to make reassembly
  unambiguous.
- Applied a second oil coat and checked the results.
- Decision: label parts before finishing, not after — see
  [`lessons-learned.md`](lessons-learned.md).
- Photos: [`photos/assembly/d04-disassembled-marked-base-1.jpg`](photos/assembly/d04-disassembled-marked-base-1.jpg),
  [`photos/assembly/d04-disassembled-marked-base-2.jpg`](photos/assembly/d04-disassembled-marked-base-2.jpg),
  [`photos/assembly/d04-disassembled-marked-base-3.jpg`](photos/assembly/d04-disassembled-marked-base-3.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d04-second-oil-coat-results-1.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d04-second-oil-coat-results-1.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d04-second-oil-coat-results-2.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d04-second-oil-coat-results-2.jpg).

## Day 5 — Legs: sanding and first coat

- Sanded the leg pieces and applied the first oil coat to them separately
  from the rest of the frame.
- Photos: [`photos/woodworking/d05-base-legs-sanding.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d05-base-legs-sanding.jpg),
  [`photos/woodworking/d05-base-legs-first-oil-coat.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d05-base-legs-first-oil-coat.jpg).

## Day 6 — Legs: second coat

- Applied the second oil coat to the legs.
- Photo: [`photos/woodworking/d06-base-legs-second-oil-coat.jpg`](photos/woodworking/d06-base-legs-second-oil-coat.jpg).

## Day 7 — Final assembly

- Assembled the finished base and placed it in its intended spot on the
  terrace.
- Fit the lower shelf (built from individual slats) to the assembled base.
- Problem encountered: the finished base position interacts with a wall
  light switch mounted nearby — visible in the final photos, worth
  accounting for when the planter box and any electronics enclosure are
  positioned.
- Result: base fully assembled, finished, and in place; ready for the
  planter box to be built and fitted to it.
- Photos: [`photos/assembly/d07-assembled-base-in-place.jpg`](photos/assembly/d07-assembled-base-in-place.jpg),
